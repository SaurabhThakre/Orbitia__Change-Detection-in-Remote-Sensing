    Algorithms Used

    PCA Working

    Change detection aims at identifying the changes in the spatial representation of any point by observing it at different times.

    It is given with the same target multiple regions or single-phase or multi-band remote sensing images using image processing methods to detect changes in surface features of the region.

    Image Segmentation Working

    Segments represent objects or parts of objects, and comprise sets of pixels, or “super-pixels”.

    Image segmentation appoints a label to each pixel in a image with the end goal that pixels with a similar label share certain attributes. It sorts pixels into larger components, eliminating the need to consider individual pixels as units of observations.

    Approaches for PCA

    Two approaches are used either post-classification analysis or difference image analysis. Post-classification comparison would first classify the contents of two temporally different images of the same scene, then compare them to identify the differences.

    Inaccurate results may arise due to errors in classification in either of the two images, thus a high degree of accuracy is required of the classification.

    Image Segmentation Accuracy

    Pixel Accuracy and mIoU are the most common two ways used to evaluate how well an image segmentation model performs.

    Intersection over Union (IoU) This method calculates the performance by calculating the intersection and union between the Ground Truth and the Prediction.
